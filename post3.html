A Comparative Study On Freeware And Licensed Software

(by Varun Solanki)

Introduction

In Order to ease up life on computer and save time and energy, there are certain applications that are required which help us to do the above. In order to use those applications, they can be freeware or payware depending upon its amount of popularity and usage. A freeware product is a product or an application that is made with open mind and one thinking that it should be free to use and not be costing any currency anywhere around the world for that product. A payware product is a product or application that is made to be bought via currency and used under a certain Agreement or License. The Agreement or License adds a restrictive use to the product. It is not compulsory that the licensed software is supposed to be payware, but most of the time it is payware. Here we will consider it as a payware software.

Let’s Get Down To The Story
The Gaming world in the 21st Century is highly sophisticated. It is so deep that if you select any certain computer game you will fall very deep into the amount of realism and hardwork that people do. In order to understand how the freeware and payware system works let’s have a look at a selective game and see how detailed things get. So get ready for an adventure!

Microsoft Flight Simulator

Started 34 years ago in 1982, Microsoft Flight Simulator (MSFS) is till date one of the oldest, educational and most played game in the computer age. The basic concept of the game is to provide the user a feel of flight, what it takes to fly a plane, something that everyone has wanted to do at least once in its lifetime. From small propeller planes to the largest sophisticated commercial airliners till the fastest military jets in the world, all types of aircraft are available on the MSFS Interface. But not only aircrafts is your option, you can choose to fly from Point A to Point B from 25,000 airports around the world in any selected time, at any selected weather. With over 15 Versions of the game in the past 34 years, Microsoft Flight Simulator X (FSX) is the latest and the last edition Microsoft ever released and stopped the production in 2009, thus ending the MSFS-Era. But it didn’t stop there. Flight Simulator fans were already very serious with the game, but due to Military Secrets, Government Records and Aviation Secrets, Microsoft never fully opened up the concept of flight to the public, so realism was never achieved 100%. In order to increase it to 100%, third party companies provided Add-ons (Additional Features) to the game for the concept “As Real As It Gets”. Here comes the topic of Payware and Freeware. First came out freeware aircrafts that increased the feeling of flying a real aircraft. But again they just touched the 90% part. So very detailed aircraft came out touching the 100% as we entered the 21st Century. Freeware Products

One of the most famous freeware product on the MSFS Platform was provided by an American-Japanese Company called Project Opensky (Posky). People at Posky were very serious. All of them were above the age of 35-40 and happily married something people don’t find in gamers. They were so serious about testing their products, that they released 7 of them with more than 20 updates and testing 350 hours on each of the products. When they launched it they announced it will be freeware forever and that it can be used for all purposes, but only cannot be uploaded without their copyright. In other words, it may be used without payment but may usually not be modified, re- distributed or reverse-engineered without the author's permission. Warren C. Daniel and Hiroshi Igami were the main two people behind posky. But due to certain limitations, Posky did not have any 100% real data about aircrafts. Even the modeling was not quiet upto real. So as every product has its downfall, this product too had one. Without a doubt, the Project Opensky team have some real gems here. These are the best freeware aircraft available.

Licensed Software/Payware Products Payware products on MSFS Platform gradually increased once passing the year 2013 as we had an advancement in the technology. More and more people got near to the concept of C programming, HTML, Java Script, due to the online schools. So one of the most famous payware products on the MSFS Platform was provided by a group of people from Canada, Belgium, Germany, Greece, Russia, South Africa and the United States, called the Precision Manuals and Development Group (PMDG) founded by real world pilot Captain Robert S. Randazzo, and when they started, they changed the history of Flight Simulator. They created such detailed aircraft add-ons for the MSFS that it caught the attention of the biggest Aerospace Giant, “The Boeing Company” and the most respected Military Company, “Lockheed Martin”. PMDG requested Boeing to give crucial and inside information about their aircrafts, and thus PMDG and Boeing joined together and changed the history of MSFS. Since Boeing gave crucial information about its aircraft to PMDG, PMDG made the most detailed versions of aircrafts for MSFS. Now since there was crucial information involved, an End-User License Agreement or EULA is signed when you buy the product. EULA is a legal contract between a software application author or publisher and the user of that application. Some copyright owners use EULAs in an effort to circumvent limitations the applicable copyright law places on their copyrights (such as the limitations in sections 107–122 of the United States Copyright Act), or to expand the scope of control over the work into areas for which copyright protection is denied by law (such as attempting to charge for, regulate or prevent private performances of a work beyond a certain number of performances or beyond a certain period of time). Such EULAs are, in essence, efforts to gain control, by contract, over matters upon which copyright law precludes control. This kind of EULAs concurs in aim with DRM and both may be used as alternate methods for widening control over software

The Thesis

Freeware products can be used however you want it to be used, But licensed products have a certain limitation. If the product is tampered with or used with any other purposes, it can cause Jail terms with high fines. In the United States, Section 117 of the Copyright Act gives the owner of a particular copy of software the explicit right to use the software with a computer, even if use of the software with a computer requires the making of incidental copies or adaptations (acts which could otherwise potentially constitute copyright infringement). In addition to granting rights and imposing restrictions on the use of copyrighted software, software licenses typically contain provisions which allocate liability and responsibility between the parties entering into the license agreement. In enterprise and commercial software transactions these terms often include limitations of liability, warranties and warranty disclaimers, and indemnity if the software infringes intellectual property rights of others.
